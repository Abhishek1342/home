What are IIITs?

The Indian IT industry is revolutionizing at a rapid rate. Everyday new technologies are developed, new ideas are nurtured or a major breakthrough is made.


To keep up with this rapidly increasing change in the Indian IT industry, Indian Institutes of Information Technology (IIITs) are set up by the Ministry of Human Resource Development, Government of India.

Who are we?

We are a community of all the students of IIITs united together for sharing news, collaborating events, and growing up together to make our institute a better place.

What's our purpose?

To create a community of all IIITians united together for sharing news in a seamless way, collaborating events and connecting with the aim of creating a better competitive environment to live in.

Objective

IIITs are model of education which can produce best-in-class human resources in IT and harnessing the multidimensional facets of IT in various domains.

Admissions

Students qualifying IIT - JEE Mains are eligible to get admissions in these technical institutes according to their ALL INDIA Rank.

Curriculum

Each lllT have self sustaining autonomy to develop its own academic programmes including curriculum, new courses and method of assessment.

  • IIITs Funding

    IIITs at Gwalior, Allahabad, Jabalpur, Kurnool and Kancheepuram are fully government funded IIITs. Rest of the IIITs are based on the public-private partnership (PPP) model, funded by the central government, state governments and industry partners in the ratio 50:35:15.

  • What is PPP model?

    A public–private partnership involves both government and private organizations working together to complete a project. New IIITs are funded by the central government, state governments and private organizations in the ratio 50:35:15.

  • IIITs Vs Other Colleges.

    IIIITs, unlike other major government funded colleges, are a group of institutes that are focused mainly on information technology. They have a curriculum strictly aligned as per the needs of the IT industry.

  • Courses

    Most of the IIITs offer undergraduate courses in Computer Science & Engineering, Electronics & Communication Engineering and Information Technology. However some IIITs also offer undergraduate courses in design (IIIT-Jabalpur), mechanical engineering (IIIT-Kancheepuram), etc.

  • No Management Quota

    No, for undergraduate admission in any IIIT, one must give the JEE-Mains exam and take part in the JOSAA counselling.

  • Curriculum

    The curriculum is strictly aligned to the needs of the IT industry. It is geared at keeping abreast with the latest developments in research and IT industry.

  • Campus

    Few IIITs have their own campus. Others are running in a temporary campus situated in some other colleges (like various NITs), until the construction of their campus is complete.
